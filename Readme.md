## 📋 Project Overview

This project addresses the complex challenge of **surgical scheduling** in a hospital environment. The primary goal is to minimize total operational costs (fixed room opening costs and overtime expenses) while maximizing schedule reliability. 

Through a series of four modeling stages, we explore various methods to handle uncertainty in surgical durations, progressing from a naive deterministic view to a sophisticated **Hybrid Stochastic-Robust** model.

### Key Problem:
- **Efficiency:** Minimize room opening costs (accounting for heavy weekend surcharges).
- **Reliability:** Ensure schedules respect the physical 10-hour daily limit to avoid procedure cancellations.
- **Uncertainty:** Surgical durations follow a uniform distribution $\mathcal{U}[t_{fixed}, t_{fixed} + \alpha \cdot t_{sup}]$.

## 📂 Project Structure

The project is structured into 4 main notebooks, representing the chronological evolution of our research:

1.  **`1 - deterministic.ipynb`**: The baseline model. Assumes fixed durations. Highly cost-efficient on paper but fails in ~60% of real-world simulations.
2.  **`2 - stochastic.ipynb`**: Implements **Sample Average Approximation (SAA)** to minimize expected costs over multiple random scenarios.
3.  **`3 - robust.ipynb`**: Implements the **Bertsimas & Sim** approach using an uncertainty budget ($\Gamma$). Identifies the critical "Dense-Packing" flaw.
4.  **`4 - hybrid.ipynb`**: Our final V2 model. It corrects the robust model's loopholes by integrating expected delays into the baseline duration.

### Output Folders:
- `/results_robust`: Contains Pareto fronts and schedules generated by the classic Robust model.
- `/results_hybrid`: Contains Excel schedules and visual analysis for the Hybrid model, categorized by severity level ($\alpha$).

## 🧠 Scientific Insights: The "Dense-Packing" Paradox

A major contribution of this project is the identification and resolution of the **Dense-Packing effect** in classic Robust Optimization. 

In a classic robust model, the solver tends to "cheat" by entassing many low-risk patients together, assuming their delay will be zero if they aren't part of the $\Gamma$ budget. In reality, these minor delays accumulate and breach the 10-hour limit. Our **Hybrid Model** (Light Robustness) solves this by adjusting every patient's baseline to their statistical expected duration, forcing the solver to distribute risk equitably across the week.

### Benchmarking (at stress level $\alpha = 1.0$):

| Model | Reliability (%) | Avg. Cost (€) | Status |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Deterministic** | ~40% | 19,850 | ❌ Unfeasible |
| **Stochastic (SAA)** | ~85% | 20,269 | ⚠️ Vulnerable to tail-risk |
| **Robust ($\Gamma=2$)** | ~99% | 20,330 | ✅ Secure |
| **Hybrid ($\Gamma=2$)** | **~99%** | **20,290** | 🏆 **Optimal Choice** |

## 📊 Managerial Recommendations

- **Optimal Strategy:** Adopt the **Hybrid Model with $\Gamma=2$**. This represents the Pareto-optimal choice, offering maximum reliability for a minimal cost increase.
- **Safety Protocol:** Escalate to **$\Gamma=3$** only during extreme stress periods where surgical complexity is expected to exceed 150% of nominal time ($\alpha \ge 1.5$).
- **Avoid Over-Protection:** Setting $\Gamma \ge 4$ is counter-productive; it triggers the dense-packing loophole, making the schedule brittle and operationally unstable.
